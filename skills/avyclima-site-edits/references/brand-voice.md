# Brand voice — avyclima.be

This file is the source of truth for any body copy written for the site (page content, blog posts, FAQ answers, meta descriptions, CTA text). When in doubt, copy phrasing from an existing page rather than inventing new wording.

## Language and register

The site is written in **Belgian Dutch** for a regional Belgian audience (East Flanders). The register is **formal-but-warm**: professional, factual, plain-language, never breezy or American-pep.

- **Always use "u" / "uw" / "uwe".** Never "je", "jij", "jou", "jouw".
- **Always Belgian Dutch idiom.** "Plaatsen" (not "installeren" except when it's the noun "installatie"), "GSM" is fine but use "telefoon" in formal contexts, "btw" lower case, decimal comma (`€ 2.500,00`), thousands separator with a dot.
- **Use the spelling "airco"** (the colloquial Belgian word) **alongside "airconditioning"** — both are correct and the site mixes them deliberately for SEO. "Klimatisatie" only in the FAQ when explaining the formal term.
- **Energie-prijs terms** in lower case: `kWh`, `m²`, `°C`. Currency before number with thin space: `€ 2.500`.
- **Region names without articles.** "in Aalst" not "in de stad Aalst". "regio Denderleeuw" or "Denderleeuw en omgeving" are common framings.

## How the company refers to itself

- **`AVYclima`** — uppercase A-V-Y, lowercase clima, no space. The HTML logo splits it as `AVY<span>clima</span>` for visual styling but it's read as one word.
- Founded 2013, based in **Denderleeuw**.
- Founder: **Ayoub Vijverman**, "Oprichter & Hoofdtechnicus".
- Always "wij / ons / onze" when speaking as the company. Never "ik" except in a quoted testimonial.
- Avoid "het team" or "ons team" filler — be specific where possible ("onze monteurs", "Ayoub", "onze technici").

## Trust signals to use freely

These are real and verified — use them by exact name, never paraphrased:

- **Erkend Daikin installateur**
- **Daikin D1+ Installer** (the highest tier — important to spell as `D1+`, not `D1 Plus` or `D1 Premier`)
- **Koeltechnisch Bekwaamheidscertificaat** (often abbreviated `KVB` after first mention) — the legal certificate required to handle refrigerant
- **VEA Erkenning** — Vlaams Energieagentschap recognition
- **F-gas certificering**
- **Sinds 2013** / **al meer dan 10 jaar ervaring**

When listing certificates, the standard order is: D1+ → KVB → VEA → F-gas.

## Differentiators the company emphasises

The site repeatedly leans on these. When writing a new section, weave at least one in:

1. **Persoonlijk advies, gratis plaatsbezoek.** Free site visit and quote, no commitment.
2. **Vrijblijvende offerte.** Quote is non-binding; transparent, no hidden costs.
3. **Erkend Daikin installateur** — not a generic HVAC firm, an exclusive Daikin specialist.
4. **Volledige nazorg en onderhoud.** End-to-end service: advies → installatie → onderhoud → herstelling.
5. **Lokaal verankerd.** Aalst / Denderleeuw / Ninove based; understands Vlaamse premies and lokale woningen.
6. **Particulieren én KMO's.** Both residential and small-business work.
7. **Energiezuinig koelen én verwarmen** — airco isn't just cooling, it's also a low-cost heat source via heat pump effect.

## Anti-patterns — don't write like this

- ❌ "Hey daar! Heb je last van de hitte?" — too casual, uses "je"
- ❌ "Bij ons sta jij centraal." — uses "jij"
- ❌ "Onze passie is jouw comfort." — corporate fluff
- ❌ "Bel ons vandaag nog en bespaar honderden euro's!" — sales-y, exclamation mark
- ❌ "Click here to get a free quote!" — not Dutch
- ❌ "We zijn de #1 airco-firma van België." — unverifiable superlative
- ❌ "100% tevredenheidsgarantie" — vague claim, avoid unless backed by a real warranty document
- ❌ "Beste prijs gegarandeerd!" — same

The voice is "competent local specialist talking to a homeowner who has done some research", not "marketer trying to close a sale".

## City pages — local content patterns

When writing a new city landing page (or refreshing one):

- Mention the city by name in the H1, the first paragraph, at least one H2, and the FAQ section.
- Acknowledge the local context where possible: type of housing (rijwoningen, fermettes, nieuwbouw), proximity to Aalst/Brussel, known industriezones if relevant.
- Reference Vlaamse premies (Mijn VerbouwPremie, EPC-premie) — these apply across Vlaanderen but the city page is where prospects land.
- Don't fabricate local stats or case studies. If there's a real portfolio entry for that city, link to it; otherwise leave the case-study slot for later.

## Blog posts — voice notes

Blog posts are slightly more conversational than service pages but still use "u". Structure:

1. Lead paragraph: state the question or scenario the post answers.
2. 2–4 H2 sections, each answering one sub-question, with practical concrete numbers where possible.
3. A short "Wat kost dat?" or "Wat moet u onthouden?" closer.
4. CTA: link to the relevant service hub + contact page.

Concrete numbers (kW, kWh, € richtprijs, COP/SCOP values) make blog posts more useful than abstract advice — include them when you can verify them.
